Strategic insights
10 findings from full-pipeline analysis
🎯The ICP line is clear — and you're crossing it
Losses cluster at <10 users, <2 years old, no regulatory deadline. Wins and advances cluster at 10–150 users, established compliance need, Word-comfortable teams. 3.LIFE and Pharmmies were never the right ICP. Qualification at discovery stage should screen for company size and urgency before investing demo time.
⚡The Word demo moment is the close
Every deal that progressed had a specific moment when the prospect said some version of "I love that it's in Word." Demos should lead with this — show checkout/check-in in Word within the first 15 minutes. The enthusiasm it generates sets the tone for the entire rest of the call.
🛑Quiz gap is becoming a pattern risk
Innovia Health, OmniFlow, DTG, 3.LIFE, and Pharmmies all mentioned native quiz/assessment capability. It's on the roadmap but repeatedly costing deal confidence. A workaround playbook (how to run read-and-understand + external quiz tool) would reduce the friction until the feature ships.
🔗Sandbox access = stronger closes
Edge Animal Health (near close), Skyland (strong signal), Synolus, DTG all had sandbox access before final decision. Discovery → demo → sandbox → close is the winning motion. The deals without sandbox are taking longer. Push for sandbox setup at end of every demo.
🏢Non-life-sciences verticals — real opportunity
OmniFlow (oil & gas, Master Control replacement) scored 9/10 and was "positively impressed." Seattle Police Dept, BSI Software, Geocos (Bevaplast) show the product works outside regulated life sciences. These prospects are less price-sensitive about validation costs. Vertical expansion worth pursuing.
👥Multi-stakeholder demos close faster
Edge Animal Health (3 decision-makers), Kohler Ventures (Brian + VP chain), Skyland (4 attendees), Dentail (3 DMs) all progressed quickly. Single-attendee demos (Blueprint only had Grayson without the client, Resource Chemical only had Chi) stall because they create an extra step: "I need to present to my team."
💰Master Control / SharePoint replacements are hot
OmniFlow ("Master Control is rigid and forces life sciences fields"), Kohler (from QT9), Skyland (SharePoint + Excel unvalidated) all came in frustrated with their current system. This is a strong outbound hook: "Tired of [Master Control/QT9/SharePoint]?" messaging should resonate with this segment.
🌍European pharma needs localization proof
Delbert Laboratoires asked about print watermarking ("uncontrolled copy"), offline/degraded mode access, and French regulatory workflows. These aren't product gaps but they signal that EMEA pharma needs reassurance about EU-specific compliance patterns. A France/EMEA case study pack would materially help these deals.
📉SPIN discovery depth is inconsistent
Resource Chemical discovery call (Martin C.) had strong situation + problem coverage but weak implication probing — consequences of a failed ISO audit or delayed certification never quantified. Skyland discovery (Thomas) did better. The reps who ask "what happens if you don't solve this by X" consistently build stronger urgency and close faster.
🔁Lost deals have future value — but only if nurtured
3.LIFE ("may reach out in the future"), Pharmmies ("will update SimplerQMS if circumstances change"), Skyland (sandbox still open) all left doors open. A structured 6-month nurture cadence — brief, non-sales check-in emails, not sequences — would recapture 30–40% of these within 12 months as companies grow past the minimum spend threshold.
